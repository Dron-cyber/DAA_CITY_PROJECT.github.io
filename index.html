Understanding the Course & Its Relevance

What is this course really about?

Algorithms are the backbone of every organized system — whether in computing or urban planning. This course teaches how large problems can be broken into logical steps, the same approach used for designing scalable, efficient cities.

What data structures & algorithms have we explored?

We explored stacks, queues, trees, tries, heaps, skip lists, and graph algorithms. Each has a real-world parallel — queues like traffic lines, trees like zoning layers, graphs like road networks — making them essential for smart city design.

How does this relate to real-world applications?

Algorithms guide navigation systems, optimize transport, manage city resources, and handle large datasets. Our city uses Dijkstra for routing, sorting for zoning, and graph-based planning for public transport and utilities.
